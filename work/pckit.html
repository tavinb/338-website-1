PCkit

About this project:

Winter 2016
Built with Adobe Illustrator

Business developed in 52 hours as part of the San Luis Obispo Startup Weekend. UX designer for “the IKEA of custom computers.”

01. Problem

My team wanted to give everyday people—fathers and sons—the ability to build their own PC. We take out the difficulty of finding parts by prompting users questions to sort through our algorithm to find the perfect combination for their need.

We worked extensively to find minimum number of questions we need prompt the user to filter through our algorithm and find best fit for their need. Everything can be boiled down to 2 simple steps:
1. Pick a game. Many games require certain machine performance.
2. Expected Graphics Quality you hope to play at.

02. Pick your games.

To effectively shave off unnecessary steps, every detail was dissected. I firmly believe sketching designs prior to using software most quickly allows for this.

Here, I experimented with different interactions to pick multiple games a user will be playing with.

03. Pick your performance.

Here, I played with different ways to represent performance (muscles, batteries, charts).
Also shown are some sketches which did not make it to production, including a tool for picking PC peripherals, and an option to upgrade certain parts beyond our suggested build.

04. Final Process

With our web app, users can purchase their own PCkit in just 6 simple steps.
